Psychology & Political Science at the University of Alberta. Interested in political theory, law, and writing / journalism.

This site began the way most things do for me: with questions. About the world, about systems, about people, and about how technology and ideas quietly shape our everyday lives.

I’m Saaim Japanwala — a student, a writer, and someone who’s constantly documenting the things I’m learning, building, and unlearning along the way. What you’ll find here isn’t a polished manifesto or a finished product. It’s a working archive. Essays, projects, code, photography, and notes that sit somewhere between curiosity and conviction.

If something here makes you pause, disagree, or think a little longer, then it’s doing its job. Not because every idea presented is meant to be accepted, but because passivity is easy and thought is not.
