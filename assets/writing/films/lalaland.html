Release and Awards

La La Land first premiered on August 31, 2016 as the opening film of the 73rd Venice International Film Festival immediately creating a strong buzz in the community. Later that year it was released in the U.S before releasing worldwide

Upon release, the film became a cultural moment. It was praised for reviving the modern musical, balancing nostalgia with contemporary themes, and it quickly dominated awards season. La La Land won seven Golden Globes and received 14 Academy Award nominations, ultimately winning six Oscars. Its release also sparked broader conversations about ambition, love, and whether prioritizing personal dreams inevitably comes at the cost of human connection. With its phenomenality it placed itself as one of the most discussed films of the 2010s.

Personal Analysis

I LOVED La La Land without hesitation. From the first frame, it feels like a film made with complete confidence in its identity, and commitment showing in every little detail. Coming up to the films 10 year anniversary, This review feels a little more special!

The casting is genuinly perfect. Ryan Gosling and Emma Stone dont just play Sebastian and Mia, they embody them almost becoming them. Their chemistry feels lived in, awkward, warm and painfully real, which makes the emotional beats hit harder.

The OST is brilliant. Justin Hurwitz’s score is unforgettable, weaving jazz, romance, and melancholy into something that lingers long after the credits roll. After 10 years of the films release, the OST still has a timeless impact, something that still invokes the same feeling as it did 10 years ago. As a musical, the heightened expression is expected, but there are moments where the “musical” aspect does feel a bit much, slightly breaking immersion. Still, those moments are minor compared to how effectively the music drives emotion and storytelling. That is where I take away 0.5 of my rating.

Visually, the film is off the charts. The camera work is fluid and intentional, often moving like it’s dancing alongside the characters. The color palette, those deep blues, warm yellows, and dreamlike purples, does more than look beautiful; it creates mood, signaling emotion before a word is spoken. Every shot feels composed with care, balancing realism with fantasy in a way that few films pull off successfully.

Overall, La La Land feels like a rare passion project that actually delivers. The performances, the music, the visuals, everything comes together to create something deeply moving and memorable. Even with its small imperfections, it’s a film that understands exactly what it wants to be, and executes it with style, heart, and ambition.
