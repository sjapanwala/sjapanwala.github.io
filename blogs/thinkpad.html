Bringing And Old Thinkpad Back To Life

Last Updated: Nov 25, 2024

Some Background Information

Thinkpads are some of the most versitile computers, with thier excellence in repairability and servicing, they have become a fan favourite around the tech community. Dating back to the IBM Thinkpad created back in the 90's as primarily an office computer. They have always exceeded in thier classes as a portable computer. Since then, Lenovo has taken over the prodution and salesof the thinkpad. Unfortunately, the modern thinkpad has strayed away from its easy repairability, servicing and, overall aesthetic, as it inches towards how modern day laptops are; garbage within 2 years. Now nerds like me love a computer like this, load up a light weight distro on the thinkpad, create some cool configs, and the computer is as good as new! (well not quite, but you get the gist). So that is exactly what I was on the quest to do!

Chapter 2
It Turns ON!... But whats wrong with it?
November 24, 2024

Well it was finally the next day, the charger was supposed to come in the mail today, and I couldnt stop checking the Amazon app to see the delivery updates. Estimated delivery times kept changing, but finally the package had arrived. I couldnt wait to play with my old toy, but the fear of a supervisor lock kept was still there and, what if it didnt turn on; it was a gamble anyways. I plug the charger in and the power light goes green! "Texts go green, it hits a little different, don't it?"" - Drake, 2022. Pressing the power button, the backlight turns on and soon enough i see the thinkpad logo pop up, but it doesnt boot into the OS i had downloaded prior . (this was me being dumb, because since the old OS was Windows, it didnt have the GRUB bootloader to boot the debian installation). After trouble shooting, I had finally booted into the Installation, everything was looking great! But was it...

Back in 1997, a great feature was created, something that would revoloutionize the way we use our personal devices, it connects us to the great pipeline of the wonderful world of internet called Wi-Fi. This thinkpad on the other hand has been having connectivity issues, where i think either the drivers have not been installed properly, or the wifi card itself is malfunctioning. "But Saaim, how did you download and configure all the other things?" and to that I say "Great Question!" while installing the dependencies, I was not able to detect any networks locally, though it detected my wifi card on installation. So i opted for a wired connection. Now the 2 suspects of this issue I think is,

  • The Drivers
    • - while installing, I chose the primary connection to be the LAN / Wired connection. so my guess is, debian never really installed the packages and drivers for Wifi or specifically my wifi card

  • The Wi-Fi Card Itself
    • - another possible problem could be, the wifi card itself is possibly malfunctiong. It could be that the wires are moved, or something it out of place. But as optimisitic as I want to be, I think the card is damaged; as its very old. since the card is being detected as a PCI input, i believe the card works, but it may not be able to detect the local networks around it.

    So whats the workaround? Thats what we figure out really really soon!
